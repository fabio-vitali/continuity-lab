# DR-0004 — Do Not Establish Engineering Continuity as a Market Category

## Context

The Product Foundation proposed **Engineering Continuity** as a category and **Engineering Continuity Control Plane** as the product type.

PF-001 searched for current category evidence and compared the proposed surface with established adjacent products. The exact phrase is used inconsistently across physical engineering, consulting, organizational continuity, and emerging agent-memory projects. No stable software category, buyer, analyst framing, or consistent competitive set was identified.

At the same time, GitHub, GitLab, Atlassian, Port, Cortex, policy engines, workflow runtimes, and coding-agent vendors increasingly cover the proposed capabilities under existing categories.

## Decision

Do not treat **Engineering Continuity** as a validated market category.

The phrase may remain an internal problem-domain label. It must not be used as evidence that the product occupies a distinct budget or competitive category.

Describe the current product hypothesis using established language:

> Repository-native, evidence-bound context and handoffs for resumable engineering work across humans and coding agents.

Final commercial naming and category creation remain open and require later market evidence.

## Status

Validated, not ratified.

## Supporting evidence

See `docs/50-validation/product-falsification-report.md`, especially:

- Market and category falsification;
- Strongest substitute stacks;
- Falsification by required question — Category.

## Alternatives considered

### Ratify Engineering Continuity as a new category

Rejected because current evidence does not establish recognized buyers, budgets, or boundaries.

### Rename immediately into an adjacent established category

Deferred because the surviving product wedge still requires empirical validation and final naming is out of scope.

### Use Engineering Continuity only as an internal domain label

Accepted.

## Risks

- Removing the category claim may weaken the apparent ambition of the product.
- Established language may make the product sound like a feature rather than a company.
- A category could emerge later and be captured by another vendor.

## Validation requirements

Before making a category claim, obtain evidence of:

- a repeated buyer problem across several organizations;
- a coherent competitive set;
- budget authority;
- language independently used by prospective adopters;
- value not reducible to one existing category or native vendor feature.

## Consequences

- Product documents no longer describe the product as an Engineering Continuity Control Plane.
- Market positioning must lead with the concrete continuation problem.
- The working name remains provisional.
